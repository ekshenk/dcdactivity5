I am a senior Digital Culture and Design major at Coastal currently. I'm super ready to graduate and just hoping to not have to come back. I play the cello, so I guess I am interested in that. I love art, although I am a terrible artist(as you have probably seen in some of my stuff throughout this class). I suck at being a student. I get really overhwelmed very easily and break under pressure, but also the pressure inspires me. It pushes me to do more and push myself harder, but I suck at it also.



To be completely honest, and I believe in honesty, I have no idea what I want to do with the rest od my life. I'm currently waiting to hear back from a job that I'm not even sure that I want because I need something after graduation. Over the years, I've jumped around aspirations. I wanted to be a doctor first, but someone told me that as a woman I wasn't smart enough and I listened. At one point I wanted to be a nurse, but blood freaks me out so that was a bad idea. Then I wanted to be an actor, then a teacher, then a writer, and then I was just clueless. That was when I found the DCD major was when I was just clueless. My hope is that I'll use the skills I've learned from DCD to do something I enjoy, but I honestly have no idea what career I want.


I also have no idea what my aesthetic is. I like light colors, but I primarily work in darker colors. I like space and stars, but I love to draw flowers and leaves and trees. My aesthetic is a mix of so many things that it is almost chaos in itself.
